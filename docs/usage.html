üéõÔ∏è Parameters Explained

CFG Scale (Guidance)

Controls how closely the AI follows your prompt.

  • Low (5-7): More creative, less accurate
  • Medium (8-12): Balanced (recommended)
  • High (15+): Very literal, may oversaturate

Sampling Steps

Number of refinement iterations.

  • 20 steps: Fast, good quality
  • 30-50 steps: Better quality
  • 50+ steps: Diminishing returns

Sampler

Algorithm for generation process.

  • Euler a: Fast, creative variations
  • DPM++ 2M Karras: High quality (recommended)
  • DDIM: Deterministic, reproducible

Resolution

Image dimensions affect quality and VRAM usage.

  • 512√ó512: Fast, low VRAM
  • 768√ó768: Balanced
  • 1024+: Requires high VRAM, may cause artifacts

Seed

Random number that ensures reproducibility.

  • -1: Random seed each time
  • Fixed number: Same result with same settings

üí° Best Practices

1. Start Small, Upscale Later

Generate at 512√ó512, then use upscaling for final high-res output. Prevents artifacts and saves VRAM.

2. Use Descriptive Language

Be specific about style, lighting, mood, and details. "Portrait photo" is vague; "close-up portrait, studio lighting, bokeh background" is specific.

3. Experiment with Parameters

Try different samplers, steps, and CFG values. Save settings that work well for your style.

4. Save Good Seeds

When you get a great result, note the seed. You can iterate on successful generations by keeping the seed and tweaking the prompt.

5. Organize Your Outputs

Rename and sort your favorite generations. Keep a library of prompts that work well.
